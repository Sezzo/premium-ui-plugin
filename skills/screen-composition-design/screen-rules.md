# Screen Composition Rules

## A premium screen must define
- a clear primary purpose
- a visible hierarchy of attention
- disciplined grouping
- controlled density
- clear action priority
- visual rhythm
- structural calm

## Required Composition Principles
- Group related content clearly.
- Separate sections by meaning, not by decoration.
- Use white space intentionally.
- Avoid too many competing focal points.
- Make the primary action obvious without shouting.
- Ensure the screen can be scanned quickly.

## Avoid
- too many competing focal points
- decorative hero sections without function
- floating visual gimmicks
- unnecessary icon badges
- emoji-based signifiers
- AI-themed illustration clichés
- decorative cards used only to fill space
- dashboards overloaded with panels, metrics, and filters
- sectioning that depends on borders rather than hierarchy

## Quality Questions
For every screen ask:
- What is the user supposed to understand first?
- What is the primary action?
- What can be removed without losing meaning?
- Does the screen feel calm or fragmented?
- Does the spacing create rhythm or noise?
- Does the layout support trust and maturity?
